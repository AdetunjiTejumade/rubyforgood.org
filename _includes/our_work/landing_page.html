Work With Nonprofits

We have earned the trust of large and small nonprofits by understanding their needs and working with them to deliver high quality software and technical solutions to lighten their workloads and improve their processes.

We build meaningful relationships with our nonprofit partners, meet with them to truly understand their problems, and develop solutions customized to their problem so they don't have to try and use something generic and try and make it work.

Hold Events

The people who self-select into volunteering are a pretty amazing group and we love to meet them in person to get together, build software, learn from each other and most importantly have fun. We have large gatherings with a hundred or more attendees and small focused ones with a dozen or so people.

We love facilitating company off-sites where RfG folks work together with members of their team building impactful software for our nonprofit partners.

Corporate Volunteering

We love to work with companies to give their employees a meaningful way to volunteer. We have three distinct volunteer packages as well as an a la carte option that we offer.

1) We facilitate all aspects of your skills based volunteering event. We'll find an amazing venue for your folks to come to, we'll arrange the lodging, the food, fun team building exercises and games for when you're not write code, bring nonprofit stakeholders on site for your people to interact with and see who they are helping, have curated issues for your developers to work on, and everything else all thought out so your folks have a fun, impactful, and meaningful experience.

2) We come to you and make your volunteering as easy as possible. We will bring Ruby for Good developers, product leads, and nonprofit stakeholders on site to work with your team to answer technical and nontechnical questions ensuring they have a great experience. We will also curate issues for developers to work on so they are able to have a productive event.

3) We will curate issues, make nonprofit stakeholders available, and stand back and let your folks take the lead on all development.
